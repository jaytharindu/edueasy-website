Scope

Introduction

Smart learning concepts are especially useful for students to learn better in large classes. In large classes, lecture method is the most popular method of teaching. In lecture method, the lecturer presents the content mostly using lecture slides and the students make their own notes based on the content presented. However, some students may find difficulties of the above method due to various issues such as speed in delivery. The purpose of this research is to assist students in large classes in the following content.


The research proposes a solution with four components namely Note Taker, Reference Finder, Question Presenter and Slide Matchers which are helpful for the students to obtain a summarized version of the lecture note, find resources, revise content using questions and easily navigate to the content.



Literature Survey

The smart learning concepts are used rapidly all over the world for better teaching and learning methods. Smart learning reflects how advanced technologies are enabling learners to digest knowledge and skills more efficiently and conveniently. Every day, researchers are seeking out for new technologies and new methods to improve smart learning concepts. So the researchers inclined towards smart learning concepts are growing more than ever before. Although the ongoing global movement towards smart learning makes and important revolution in modern learning, many people still find these concepts vague.


Society is normally resistant to change. Since smart learning is in developing stages, it faces several barriers such as rigid nature of traditional education, insufficient time to learn e-learning tools and implement them into teaching practice, lack of recognition, teachers’ low self-efficiency in educational technology knowledge, shortage of knowledge and motivation among students etc. However, smart learning can offer many benefits to the academic community. These benefits include increased productivity, interactive and enhanced learning experience, simplified teaching, cost reduction etc. The academic community and public at large, should embrace smart learning as solution to many problems.


Despite all the issues and complications, researches and applications inclined towards smart learning and e-learning are growing rapidly. For examples, researches have conducted about automatic speech transcriptions, automatic text summarization, intelligent education assistant chatbots, automatic question generation etc. Also there are several applications developed for smart learning such as LinguaKit, GoConqr, SlidePlayer etc. to help students do their daily tasks.



Research Gap

Although much research has been carried out and several systems developed about smart learning, there’s no simple, cohesive concept or a product of smart learning. The purpose of this research is to provide solutions for some major difficulties university students experience during the lecture room environment. The proposed system, EduEasy Smart Learning Assistant System is capable of providing four main services.


First one is The Note Taker. What this component basically does is transcribe the lecturer’s voice and generate a summary. Then the summarized note can be uploaded to an online database so that students can refer it later. Second component, The Reference Finder is designed to search every type of reference material all over the internet using identified keywords from the previously mentioned summarized note.can find online questions related to their course. This component is designed to search the internet for related questions and present them in one place. Fourth component, The Slide Matcher can compare the selected topic or subtopic with the lecture notes which are stored in the cloud database. Students can use this feature to refer the lecture slides whenever they want while using the summarized note.



Research Problem

In teaching and learning, teaching materials and the support materials play an important role. In traditional teaching and learning, teacher may present materials in form of lecture slides and the students listens to the lecture, take notes, study them and practice application of knowledge by answering questions.


However, students face many challenges during the above process. For examples, the students’ note may not be complete due to several issues such as teacher’s accent and the speed of speech, most of the students skip the process of referring relevant reference materials because it is difficult to find suitable references, students struggles to find practice questions relevant to course contents because of their tight schedules it is difficult to search different sources for suitable questions etc.



Research Objectives

Main Objective

  • Develop an E-leaning application for students to effectively learn and revise lectures done at the university.

Specific Objectives

  • Capture the lecturer’s voice using microphone and voice recorder.
  • Develop a speech-to-text converter to transcribe lectures into text.
  • Develop a text summarizer to summarize the transcription.
  • Conduct a research/survey to find out the most effective text summarization algorithm among popular algorithms.
  • Develop an attractive and easy to use user interface for the speech-to-text converter and the text summarizer.
  • Deploy the web application.

Methodology

Students who study smart instead of study hard, can have good success in their education. But studying smartly is a challenge without the correct support. EduEasy is an E-learning application for students to effectively learn and revise lectures done at university. EduEasy application has mainly four components. They are Note Taker, Reference Finder, Question Presenter and Slide Matcher. Each of those components will help to overcome problems which university students has to face on a daily basis. The figure on the left demonstrates an overview of the EduEasy system.


The Note Taker component records lecturer’s voice to the computer. Then using a transcription algorithm, audio is converted into text format and that transcribed text is converted into a summarized note using a summarization algorithm. Using that summarized note, the Reference Finder component can find exact relevent reference materials and the Question Presenter component can generate and find questions relevent to the note. The Slide Matcher component can compare a database of lecture slide with the summarized note and find the most suitable lecture slides for the note.
